The Reality

Every day, animals on the East End need somewhere to go

You care about animals. You've probably even thought about doing more. But without a clear, easy way to help, that feeling stays just a feeling. Here's what's actually happening in our community right now.

🏠

Financial crisis leads to surrender

More animals arrive at ARF not because of behavior issues, but because their families lost housing or income. These are loved pets with nowhere to go.

📦

Southern shelters are at capacity

ARF transports dogs and cats from overcrowded high-risk shelters in the South, where euthanasia is the alternative. Every transport costs money. Every month.

💊

Medical care never stops

Rescued animals arrive with injuries, illness, and trauma. ARF provides the vet care, behavioral support, and training they need to be adoption-ready — no matter how long it takes.

Why ARF Hamptons

A 50-year track record. A no-kill promise. Real impact you can see.

ARF Hamptons is not just a shelter. It's a full-service rescue, rehabilitation, and community support organization that has served Long Island's East End since 1974. Founded because three determined women refused to look the other way at abandoned animals, ARF has grown into one of the region's most trusted no-kill sanctuaries.

With a state-of-the-art facility, a dedicated training center, low-cost vet clinics, and a nearly 30-year trap-neuter-return program, ARF is built for the long haul. And so are the animals in its care.
